# Zitolyte Family — Marketing Narrative

*(Read aloud word for word.)*

---

In a sky that has forgotten what silence sounds like, one name cuts through the thunder.

Zitolyte.

Not a fleet. Not a family. A frontier.

Meet the Z-89 Ducksmasher. Six Spiro homing missiles. Twelve hundred-megaton Goliath improvised cobalt bombs. Mach eighty-nine. When the Ducksmasher arrives, the horizon moves out of the way.

Meet the Z-100 Duckfighter. Twelve Diablo zyto-powered missiles. Mach one hundred. Faster than the warning. Faster than the fear.

Need to move an army before breakfast? The Z-5 Zyto-lifter carries your people, your payload, and fourteen Fakir air-to-air missiles — at Mach five, in full zitolyte suits. Arrive ready. Arrive untouched.

Looking for answers? The Z-150 Zito-streak takes them at Mach one hundred fifty, escorted by ten Sultan zitolyte-powered missiles. It does not knock.

From the deck of any carrier, the Z-95 Murderer launches with ten Okanogan homing missiles at Mach ninety-five. The name is the mission statement.

Close to the ground, closer to the target: the Z-2 Zyto-chopper. Five Rajak air-to-surface missiles — and a zitolyte-powered submarine surface-to-air atomicle missile. One rotor. Every dimension.

And when the mission is speed alone, the Z-1 Zyto-cruiser runs light — two air-to-air missiles, thirty-two thousand miles per hour. The trailer that outruns the pursuit.

One family. Every altitude. Every ocean. Every outcome.

Zitolyte. Powered by tomorrow. Answerable to no one.

*— Isaih*
