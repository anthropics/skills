# Brand Voice Examples

These before/after examples show how the Brand Voice Enforcer transforms generic, flat,
or off-brand content into writing that sounds human, specific, and consistent with a creator's voice.

---

## Example 1: LinkedIn Post (Direct / No-Fluff Voice)

**Voice Profile:** Direct, no-nonsense, hates corporate speak, audience is startup founders

**Before (off-brand):**
> It's important to leverage synergies across your organisation to empower teams and unlock value at scale. In today's fast-paced business environment, holistic approaches to leadership are transformative.

**After (on-brand):**
> Most leadership advice is vague on purpose.
>
> "Empower your teams." Cool. How?
>
> Here's what actually worked for us at [Company]:
> We killed the weekly status meeting and replaced it with a written update every Friday. Response optional.
>
> Two months later, engineers shipped 40% faster.
>
> Try that before you read another leadership book.

**What changed:** Cut every buzzword. Added a specific outcome. Gave a concrete action. Used the creator's direct, slightly provocative tone.

## Example 2: Newsletter Intro (Warm / Conversational Voice)

**Voice Profile:** Warm, personal, like a letter from a trusted friend, audience is indie creators

**Before (off-brand):**
> Welcome to this week's edition of the newsletter. In this issue, we will be covering several important topics related to building an online business as a creator.

**After (on-brand):**
> I almost didn't send this one.
>
> Not because I didn't have anything to say — but because what I wanted to say felt too honest. Too unfinished.
>
> Then I remembered that's exactly why you're here.
>
> So here goes.

**What changed:** Removed the formal newsletter framing. Created immediate intimacy and tension. Sounds like a real person, not a content schedule.

## Example 3: Tweet (Funny / Punchy Voice)

**Voice Profile:** Dry humour, short punchy takes, audience is developers and tech people

**Before (off-brand):**
> I've been thinking about the importance of work-life balance and how it's essential for long-term productivity and mental health. It's something we should all prioritise.

**After (on-brand):**
> me at 9pm: just one more bug fix
> me at 2am: I have rewritten the entire authentication system and I need to talk to someone

**What changed:** Completely reformatted as a relatable two-liner that the audience will recognise immediately. The original message (overworking) is still there — just delivered in the voice.

## Example 4: Instagram Caption (Aspirational / Visual Voice)

**Voice Profile:** Aspirational but grounded, not toxic positivity, audience is young women building careers

**Before (off-brand):**
> Excited to share this new opportunity with you all! So grateful for this amazing journey and all the incredible people who have supported me. Truly blessed. #grateful #blessed #motivated

**After (on-brand):**
> Three years ago I was refreshing my inbox waiting for someone to give me a shot.
>
> Today I'm building the thing I was waiting to be hired into.
>
> It didn't happen because I believed harder. It happened because I kept showing up when I didn't feel like it.
>
> That's the whole secret, honestly.

**What changed:** Removed hollow gratitude performance. Replaced with a real story that earns the emotional payoff. No generic hashtags.

## Example 5: Voice Audit Output

**Task:** Audit this paragraph for brand voice consistency

**Creator's Voice Profile:** Clear, confident, slightly irreverent, never preachy

**Content submitted:**
> As creators, we must remember that our audience trusts us deeply, and it is our responsibility to ensure that the content we produce aligns with their values and expectations. We should always strive to be authentic.

**Audit Output:**

> **Voice Score: 4/10 — Needs Work**
>
> 🔴 "we must remember" — Preachy. Your voice never lectures.
> 🔴 "it is our responsibility" — Stiff and formal. Not you.
> 🔴 "we should always strive" — Vague. Strive to do what exactly?
> 🟡 "trusts us deeply" — Good instinct, but undersells it. Be specific about what that trust looks like.
>
> **Suggested rewrite:**
> Your audience came back. That's the whole trust signal. Don't waste it by publishing something you wouldn't actually stand behind.
