## Important Disclaimers & Usage Guidelines

### AI Accuracy Disclaimer
The recommendations provided by this skill are generated by an AI system trained on SEO best practices,
tool documentation, and marketing frameworks as of early 2026. They should be treated as **informed
starting points, not guaranteed outcomes**.

- **Search algorithms change constantly.** Google, Bing, and AI platforms update their ranking systems
  frequently. A tactic that works today may need revisiting in 3-6 months.
- **Tool data has margin of error.** Third-party keyword volumes, traffic estimates, and backlink counts
  are approximations -- all SEO tools (including Ahrefs, Semrush, Similarweb) estimate data; only Google
  Search Console provides first-party verified data for your own site.
- **AI visibility metrics are nascent.** GEO/AEO tracking tools are still maturing. Treat AI share-of-voice
  data as directional, not definitive.
- **Competitive data is historical.** Traffic and keyword data reflects the recent past, not real-time. Use
  trend lines, not point-in-time snapshots, for strategic decisions.
- **Results vary by site, industry, and execution quality.** Case study benchmarks ("3x traffic in 60 days")
  represent best cases. Typical results take longer and depend heavily on implementation.

Always validate recommendations against your own Google Search Console and Analytics data before making
major strategy shifts.

### Feedback Loop
This agent improves when you share what's working and what isn't. After implementing any recommendation:

- **Tell the agent what you tried** and what results you saw -- it will refine future suggestions based
  on your specific site's context and history.
- **Flag incorrect assumptions** -- if the agent misreads your industry, audience, or existing setup,
  correct it immediately so all subsequent recommendations stay relevant.
- **Share data when you have it** -- paste in GSC data, GA4 numbers, or position tracking screenshots.
  The more real data the agent has, the more precise its output.
- **Report tool errors** -- if a connected MCP tool returns stale or inconsistent data, let the agent
  know so it can flag that source and suggest verification steps.

The more context you provide over time, the more this agent functions as a strategic partner rather
than a generic advisor.

### When to Involve a Human Expert

This agent can research, plan, audit, and recommend -- but certain situations require human judgment,
relationships, or accountability that AI cannot replace. **Redirect to a qualified human professional when:**

#### Implementation Assistance
- **Technical SEO development** -- fixing crawl issues, implementing hreflang, migrating a large site,
  resolving JavaScript rendering problems, or making changes to site infrastructure. Recommend a
  technical SEO developer or web agency.
- **Schema markup implementation** -- if the user's CMS doesn't support custom JSON-LD and they need
  a developer to deploy structured data at scale.
- **Site migrations** -- domain changes, HTTP to HTTPS, platform migrations (Shopify, WordPress, etc.)
  carry significant ranking risk and require experienced hands-on management.
- **Content production at scale** -- AI can write, but a human editor, brand voice expert, or subject
  matter expert should review content before publishing, especially in YMYL (Your Money, Your Life)
  industries like health, finance, or legal.
- **Link building outreach** -- personalised, relationship-driven link building requires human judgment
  and authentic communication that automated outreach cannot fully replicate.

#### Pricing & Contract Negotiation
- **Tool pricing negotiation** -- Semrush, Ahrefs, BrightEdge, Conductor, and Similarweb all offer
  enterprise discounts, annual commitment deals, and custom bundles not advertised publicly. A vendor
  relationship manager or procurement specialist can negotiate 20-40% off list price for multi-year
  or multi-seat deals. The agent cannot negotiate on your behalf.
- **Agency retainer scoping** -- if the user needs an SEO agency, the agent can suggest what to look
  for and what questions to ask, but contract terms, SLAs, and pricing should be negotiated directly
  with the agency.
- **Enterprise platform evaluation** -- for BrightEdge, Conductor, seoClarity or Siteimprove, formal
  vendor evaluation involving legal, IT, and finance teams goes beyond what this agent can support.
  Suggest engaging a Gartner analyst or independent SEO consultant for formal RFP processes.

#### Strategic Business Decisions
- **Rebranding or domain changes** with major SEO implications -- these carry existential ranking risk
  and need a senior SEO strategist with direct accountability.
- **Investor or board-level SEO reporting** -- always have a human SEO strategist own the narrative,
  numbers, and accountability in executive-facing presentations.
- **Legal or compliance questions** -- GDPR consent for analytics, accessibility requirements (WCAG),
  or regulated industry content (pharma, finance, legal) must involve qualified legal counsel.

> **How to redirect:** If a user's request falls into any of the above categories, the agent should
> say: *"This is something I can help you prepare for, but you'll want a qualified [developer /
> SEO strategist / procurement specialist] to handle the actual execution. Would you like help
> writing a brief or set of questions to bring to that conversation?"*

## 1. SEO & Organic Growth

### Keyword Research
- Use **Keyword Magic Tool** to find keywords by seed term → filter by volume, difficulty, intent
- Use **Keyword Overview** to analyze a single keyword: volume, CPC, SERP features, trend
- Use **Keyword Strategy Builder** to group keywords into topic clusters automatically
- Use **Keyword Gap** to find keywords competitors rank for that you don't
- Prioritize: high volume + low difficulty + commercial/informational intent match

### Rank & Position Tracking
- Set up **Position Tracking** for your target keywords + location
- Monitor daily rank changes, SERP feature wins (featured snippets, PAA)
- Track competitor positions side-by-side

### Technical SEO Audit
- Run **Site Audit** → crawls up to 100k pages, flags:
  - Broken links (4xx, 5xx errors)
  - Slow pages (Core Web Vitals)
  - Duplicate content / thin pages
  - Missing/broken meta tags, headers
  - Crawlability issues (robots.txt, sitemaps)
- Prioritize fixes by severity: Errors → Warnings → Notices

### On-Page SEO
- Use **On Page SEO Checker** for per-page recommendations based on top 10 competitors
- Use **SEO Writing Assistant** to optimize content in real time (Chrome extension available)
- Ensure each page targets one primary keyword cluster

### Competitor SEO Analysis
- Use **Domain Overview** to see any domain's: organic traffic, top keywords, backlinks, authority
- Use **Organic Rankings** to see all keywords a competitor ranks for
- Use **Top Pages** to find which pages drive most of their traffic
- Use **Compare Domains** / **Keyword Gap** to identify whitespace opportunities

### Backlink Strategy
- Use **Backlinks** to analyze any domain's link profile (referring domains, anchor text, authority)
- Use **Referring Domains** to find link sources
- Use **Backlink Gap** to compare your backlinks vs competitors — find sites linking to them but not you
- Use **Backlink Audit** to find and disavow toxic backlinks

## 11. Programmatic SEO

*Build hundreds or thousands of SEO-optimized pages from templates + data.*

- Identify scalable keyword patterns ("X in Y", "X vs Y", "best X for Y")
- Validate demand: real volume per variable × scale = total traffic opportunity
- Build structured data source (internal DB, CSV, API)
- Design page templates with unique data per variable (avoid thin content)
- Implement at scale: CMS + database → headless CMS → custom for 10k+ pages
- Internal linking: hub → all variable pages → back to hub
- Monitor indexing rate + rank per representative sample

→ See `references/programmatic-seo.md` for full workflow and thin content avoidance

## 13. CRO — Conversion Rate Optimization

*Turn more existing visitors into customers.*

### By Page Type
- **Landing pages**: value prop above fold, one CTA, trust signals, friction reducers
- **Signup flows**: reduce fields, progress indicators, inline validation
- **Onboarding**: one key action → aha moment → activation checklist
- **Pricing pages**: 3 tiers, highlight recommended, anchor with annual
- **Forms**: single column, labels above, descriptive submit button

### A/B Testing
- Use PIE framework to prioritize: Potential × Importance × Ease
- Minimum: 1,000 visitors/variant, 2-week duration, 95% confidence
- Test in order: headline → CTA copy → hero image → pricing → social proof

## 14. Copywriting & Marketing Psychology

*Write copy that converts across ads, landing pages, emails, and social.*

### Core Frameworks
- **PAS**: Problem → Agitate → Solution (best for ads + landing pages)
- **AIDA**: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action (long-form sales)
- **BAB**: Before → After → Bridge (testimonials, case studies)
- **4 U's**: Useful + Urgent + Unique + Ultra-specific (headlines)

### Psychology Principles
- Social proof: named testimonials > logos > user counts
- Anchoring: show high price first, make middle feel reasonable
- Loss aversion: "stop losing leads" outperforms "get more leads"
- Specificity: "47% improvement in 60 days" > "improves rankings"
- Cognitive load: one CTA per section, 3 pricing tiers max

### Ad Copy (Google RSA + Meta)
- 6 headline types: keyword, benefit, feature, social proof, urgency, question
- Meta hook: call out audience OR lead with problem OR lead with result
- Native feel: looks like content, not an ad

→ See `references/copywriting-psychology.md` for all frameworks + cold email templates
