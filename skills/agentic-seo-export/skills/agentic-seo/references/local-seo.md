# Local SEO — Step-by-Step Guide

## Why Local SEO is Different

Local SEO targets searches with local intent:
- "pizza near me"
- "dentist in [city]"
- "plumber [neighborhood]"

**Key ranking factors (Google Local Pack):**
1. Proximity — how close you are to the searcher
2. Relevance — how well your listing matches the query
3. Prominence — how well-known/trusted your business is

---

## Google Business Profile (GBP) Optimization

### Initial Setup Checklist
- [ ] Claim and verify your GBP listing
- [ ] Choose the most accurate primary category
- [ ] Add all relevant secondary categories
- [ ] Business name: exact legal name (no keyword stuffing)
- [ ] Address: consistent with your website and directories
- [ ] Phone: local number preferred
- [ ] Website URL: link to most relevant page
- [ ] Hours: accurate + holiday hours
- [ ] Description: 750 chars, include keywords naturally
- [ ] Photos: minimum 10 (exterior, interior, team, products)
- [ ] Services/Products: fill out completely with descriptions

### Using GBP AI Agent (Semrush)
The AI Agent automates:
- **Weekly posts** → keeps profile active (Google rewards activity)
- **Review replies** → responds to all reviews within hours
- **Photo updates** → adds new images regularly
- **Info updates** → detects and fixes inconsistencies

Setup: Connect GBP account → set posting frequency + tone → let agent run

## Review Strategy

### Getting More Reviews
- **Timing**: Ask within 24–48 hours of purchase/service
- **Channel**: SMS and email campaigns (Semrush Review Generation)
- **Make it easy**: Direct link to GBP review page
- **Volume goal**: 10+ reviews/month minimum for competitive markets

### Responding to Reviews
- **Positive reviews**: Thank + reinforce positive sentiment (mention service)
- **Negative reviews**: Apologize + offer to resolve offline
- **Never argue** in public responses
- **Response time**: Under 24 hours is ideal

AI Response (Semrush): Generates brand-appropriate responses at scale

## Map Rank Tracker (Grid Ranking)

**What it shows**: Your rank at different geographic points on a grid around your location

**Why it matters**: A business might rank #1 for customers 0.5 miles away but #7 for those 2 miles away

**How to use**:
1. Set up your business + target keywords
2. Configure grid size (e.g., 5x5, 7x7) + radius
3. Run ranking check
4. Identify: where are you strong? Where are gaps?
5. Strategies to expand rank radius:
   - More reviews from customers in target areas
   - Content mentioning nearby neighborhoods
   - Local citations in target area publications

## Local Content Strategy

### Location Pages (For Multi-Location)
Each location needs its own page with:
- Unique content (not copy-paste)
- Local landmarks, neighborhoods mentioned
- Location-specific testimonials
- Embedded Google Map
- Local schema markup (LocalBusiness)

### Local Blog Content
- "[City] guide to [your service]"
- "Best [your category] in [neighborhood]"
- "[Local event] + your business connection"
- Customer stories featuring local context

---

## Local Schema Markup

Add `LocalBusiness` schema to every location page:

Key fields:
- `name`, `address`, `telephone`
- `openingHours`
- `geo` (lat/long coordinates)
- `aggregateRating` (if showing reviews)
- `priceRange`
- `servesCuisine` (restaurants) or industry-specific type

Use Google's Rich Results Test to validate.
