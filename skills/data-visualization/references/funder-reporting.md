# Nonprofit Funder Reporting — Visualization Guide

## Standard Funder Report Structure

1. **Executive Summary** — 3–5 KPI cards: key headline numbers at a glance
2. **Goal–Metric–Actual–Variance Table** — see template below
3. **Narrative + Data Integration** — chart followed by 2–3 sentence interpretation
4. **Equity Lens** — disaggregated outcomes by demographics or geography
5. **Lessons Learned** — qualitative callout box, not a chart

## Goal–Metric–Actual–Variance Table Template

| Goal | Indicator | Target | Actual | Variance | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Increase math proficiency | % students at/above grade level | 65% | 61% | -4pts | ⚠ Near |
| Deliver 40 PD sessions | # sessions completed | 40 | 47 | +7 | ✅ Met |

Status icons: ✅ Met · ⚠ Near (within 5%) · ❌ Not Met · 🔄 In Progress

## Common Funder Visual Preferences

| Funder Type | Visual Preference |
|---|---|
| **United Way** | Progress-to-goal bars; community-level aggregation |
| **Gates Foundation** | Evidence tier labeling; disaggregated equity data |
| **Federal (ED, HHS)** | Formal tables + ESSA-aligned charts; suppress small N |
| **Local/Community Foundation** | Simple, accessible charts; plain-language callouts preferred |
| **Corporate (CSR)** | Infographic-forward; headline numbers large and bold |

## Design Rules for Funder Reports

- Lead with **impact numbers**, not process metrics (funders want outcomes, not activities)
- Every chart must have a **"So What?" sentence** — a plain-language interpretation written for a non-specialist reader
- Use the **funder's own goal language** verbatim in labels and titles
- If targets were not met, include a brief explanation adjacent to the chart — never leave a miss unaddressed
- Include a **data methodology footnote** on any outcome chart (source, date collected, N, limitations)
