# Budgeting & Financial Planning

## Annual Budgeting Process

### Timeline (Reverse Engineer from Fiscal Year Start)
- **Month −4**: Strategic plan review; set top-level revenue and headcount targets
- **Month −3**: FP&A distributes departmental budget templates; finance business partners meet with functional leaders
- **Month −2**: Department heads submit bottom-up plans; FP&A consolidates and reconciles vs. top-down targets
- **Month −1**: CFO and CEO reconciliation sessions; board review draft; scenario planning (base / upside / downside)
- **Month 0**: Board approval; communicate to department heads; load into financial system

### Budget Structure
- **Revenue model**: Bottoms-up ARR bridge (beginning ARR + new bookings by segment − churn = ending ARR → recognized revenue)
- **Headcount plan**: Primary driver of OpEx; plan by department, role, and timing (backfill vs. net new)
- **Fully-loaded cost per FTE**: Salary + benefits (~20–30% of salary) + equipment (~$3K/year) + software licenses (~$2K/year) + office space allocation
- **Discretionary spend**: Travel, marketing programs, events, training — often cut first in downturns
- **Capex**: Software development capitalization plan, hardware, leasehold improvements

### Budget Approval Governance
- Line item authority: Define dollar thresholds for CEO, CFO, VP-level approvals
- Headcount freeze: Require CFO sign-off for any unplanned hire
- Variance alerts: Trigger automatic review if any department exceeds budget by >10% in a month

## Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB)

ZBB starts each planning cycle from zero — every dollar of spending must be justified from first principles, not carried forward from last year's budget.

### When to Use ZBB
- Post-fundraise with new strategic priorities
- Post-acquisition integration
- During financial distress or restructuring
- When cost structure has grown without review (common in hypergrowth)

### ZBB Process
1. Define decision units (departments or cost centers)
2. Each manager creates decision packages for their spending:
   - Minimum level: absolute minimum to deliver core function
   - Current level: status quo
   - Enhanced level: incremental investment for incremental outcome
3. Rank decision packages across the organization against strategic priorities
4. Fund from top until budget is exhausted
5. Document every spending decision with business justification

### ZBB vs. Incremental Budgeting
- ZBB advantage: eliminates waste embedded in baseline; aligns spend with current strategy
- ZBB disadvantage: time-intensive; can demotivate teams; not suited for fast-moving orgs
- Hybrid: Apply ZBB to G&A and fixed costs every 2–3 years; use rolling forecasts for revenue and variable costs

## Rolling Forecasts

A rolling forecast replaces the static annual budget with a continuously updated projection — typically 4, 6, or 8 quarters forward.

### Structure
- Update monthly or quarterly
- Extend the horizon: as Q1 closes, add Q5 to maintain a constant 4-quarter lookahead
- Separate what is known (committed pipeline, signed contracts, payroll) from what is estimated

### Advantages Over Annual Budget
- Incorporates actual performance; avoids "managing to an obsolete number"
- Forces discussion of strategic resource allocation every quarter, not just annually
- Better for investors and boards who want forward visibility, not just variance to original budget
- Particularly valuable in high-uncertainty environments (early-stage, economic volatility)

### Rolling Forecast KPIs
- Forecast accuracy: track actual vs. forecast at 30, 60, 90 days prior to period close; target ±5% for revenue, ±8% for expenses
- Bias: are forecasts consistently optimistic or pessimistic? Audit systematically.

## Variance Analysis

Variance = Actual − Budget (or Forecast)
- Positive variance on revenue: favorable (beat)
- Positive variance on expense: unfavorable (overspend)

### Framework: Price × Volume Decomposition
For revenue variances:
- **Price variance** = (Actual Price − Budgeted Price) × Actual Volume
- **Volume variance** = (Actual Volume − Budgeted Volume) × Budgeted Price
- This separates "we charged more" from "we sold more"

For headcount-driven expense variances:
- **Rate variance** = (Actual Salary − Budgeted Salary) × Actual Headcount
- **Volume variance** = (Actual HC − Budgeted HC) × Budgeted Salary

### Variance Reporting
Report monthly, within 5 business days of month-end:
1. **Flash report** (Days 1–3): Revenue, bookings, cash position, net burn — key metrics only
2. **Full variance report** (Days 5–10): Comprehensive P&L vs. budget/forecast with narrative
3. **Root cause analysis**: For any variance >5% of budget line, explain: Was it timing? Structural? Controllable?
4. **Forward look**: Update forecast based on learnings from variance

## Finance Business Partnering

The finance business partner (FBP) model embeds finance expertise within business functions (Sales, Marketing, Engineering, Product, etc.).

### FBP Responsibilities
- Own the financial model for each department: headcount plan, budget, forecast
- Translate business decisions into financial implications (e.g., "hiring 10 engineers in Q3 adds $X to run rate")
- Attend department leadership meetings; provide real-time financial insight
- Identify cost optimization opportunities proactively
- Drive accountability: present actuals vs. budget in leadership reviews

### FBP Best Practices
- Build trust with functional leaders before diving into cost scrutiny
- Lead with business insight ("your S&M efficiency is declining; here's why and what to do") not pure accounting
- Use operational data (pipeline, headcount, ticket volume) not just financial data
- Show impact of trade-offs: "investing $500K in this program generates X in pipeline; at our current conversion and CAC, that's Y new ARR"

## Departmental Budget Management: Cost Categories

| Category | % of Revenue (Growth-Stage SaaS) | Key Lever |
|---|---|---|
| S&M | 30–50% | Headcount, programs, CAC efficiency |
| R&D | 20–30% | Engineering headcount, capitalization rate |
| G&A | 10–20% | Finance, legal, HR, facilities |
| COGS | 15–30% | Infrastructure, support, PS |

**Headcount is the largest cost driver** — typically 60–75% of total OpEx. Manage it with a rolling headcount plan by quarter (starts, backfills, open roles). Track fully-loaded cost, not just salary.
