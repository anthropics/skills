# Investor Relations & Financial Communication

## Investor Update Best Practices

### Frequency and Format by Stage
| Stage | Frequency | Format | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-Seed / Seed | Monthly | Email | 300–500 words |
| Series A | Monthly | Email or brief deck | 500–800 words |
| Series B+ | Monthly metrics, quarterly narrative | Email + metrics table | 800–1,200 words |
| Public company | Quarterly earnings call | Press release + slides + call | Formal IR |

### Anatomy of a Strong Monthly Investor Update
1. **Subject line**: Include month/year and 1 headline metric (e.g., "September 2024 Update — ARR hits $12M")
2. **Header snapshot** (3–5 metrics in bold): MRR, MoM growth %, Cash/Runway, Headcount, NRR or Churn Rate
3. **Highlights (2–3 bullets)**: What went well? Quantify.
4. **Lowlights (1–2 bullets)**: Be honest. What didn't work? What are you doing about it?
5. **Key metrics table**: Full monthly metrics (see below)
6. **Product updates (optional, brief)**: Major launches or milestones
7. **Asks**: 2–3 specific requests from the investor network (introductions, references, candidates)

### Monthly Metrics Table Template
| Metric | This Month | Last Month | vs. Budget |
|---|---|---|---|

## Board Deck Financial Sections

### Financial Section Structure (Quarterly Board Deck)
1. **P&L Summary**: Revenue, Gross Profit (with margin %), EBITDA (with margin %), Net Income; vs. prior quarter, prior year, and budget; key drivers of variances
2. **ARR Bridge**: Visual waterfall: beginning ARR → new → expansion → contraction → churn → ending ARR; annotate each component with color code and commentary
3. **Cash and Liquidity**: Cash balance trend (12 months), burn rate trend (3-month rolling average), runway, any covenant compliance status
4. **Headcount**: Total FTEs vs. plan, by department; key open roles; cost per FTE trend
5. **Unit Economics**: Gross margin trend, CAC payback by segment, NRR trend, LTV:CAC
6. **Forecast**: Updated quarter-end and year-end forecast vs. budget; scenario table (bear/base/bull); key assumptions

### Board Communication Principles
- **Lead with the insight, not the data**: Not "Revenue was $X" but "We beat Q3 by 8%, driven by enterprise expansion revenue which is 40% above plan — this validates our upmarket motion"
- **Present problems proactively**: Boards despise surprises; flag risks early with mitigation plans
- **Quantify future actions**: "We expect these measures to reduce churn by 200bps in Q4" — not "we are working on churn"
- **One recommendation per issue**: Don't just diagnose — prescribe; the board wants to know what you recommend
- **Governance hygiene**: Include consent agenda (routine approvals), prior period minutes approval; keep board informed of cap table changes, legal matters, key executive changes

## Due Diligence Financial Preparation

### Virtual Data Room (VDR) Structure for Fundraise
```
/Financial Statements
    /Audited (last 2-3 years)
    /Monthly P&L (last 24 months)
    /Balance Sheet (last 24 months)
    /Cash Flow Statement (last 24 months)
/Revenue Metrics
    /ARR Bridge (monthly, 24 months)
    /MRR by Customer (sanitized with NDA)
    /NRR Cohort Analysis
    /Bookings Data (ACV by quarter, by segment)
/Unit Economics
    /CAC Model by Channel
    /LTV by Cohort
    /Payback Period Analysis
/Headcount and Compensation
    /Org Chart
    /Headcount by Department (monthly)
    /Compensation Benchmarking
/Contracts
    /Material Customer Contracts (top 10-20 customers)
    /Material Vendor Contracts
/Legal
    /Cap Table (fully diluted, with option pool detail)
    /Prior Financing Docs
    /IP Assignments
/Budget and Forecast
    /Current Year Budget
    /3-Year Financial Model
    /Key Assumptions Documentation
```

### Questions Every Investor Will Ask (Prepare Answers in Advance)
- What is your NRR by cohort and by segment (SMB vs. Enterprise)?
- What is your CAC payback period, and how has it trended?
- Who are your top 10 customers by ARR? What % of ARR do they represent?
- What is your churn by reason code? What actions have you taken and what results have you seen?
- Walk me through your path to Rule of 40 / FCF positive — what are the leverage points?
- What is the competitive landscape and how do you differentiate on price and value?
- What are the key risks to your revenue forecast?

## Metrics Benchmarking: Industry Standards

### Primary Sources (Update annually)
- **Bessemer Venture Partners State of the Cloud**: Annual report on public and private SaaS benchmarks; NRR, gross margin, growth rates by ARR band
- **OpenView Partners SaaS Benchmarks Survey**: Annual private company survey; CAC payback, NRR, Rule of 40, product-led growth metrics
- **Benchmarkit (KeyBanc Capital Markets / Sapphire)**: Annual survey of 500+ private SaaS companies; comprehensive benchmark across all KPIs
- **SaaS Capital Index**: Private SaaS company valuation benchmarks; revenue multiples for subscription businesses
- **Battery Ventures Metrics that Matter**: Annual report with growth and efficiency benchmarks for venture-backed companies

### 2024–2026 Benchmark Reference Table (Growth-Stage Private SaaS)

| Metric | Bottom Quartile | Median | Top Quartile |
|---|---|---|---|
| ARR YoY Growth | <30% | 40–60% | >80% |
| NRR (Net Revenue Retention) | <95% | 105–115% | >120% |
| GRR (Gross Revenue Retention) | <80% | 85–90% | >92% |
| Gross Margin (subscription) | <65% | 70–78% | >80% |
| CAC Payback Period | >24 months | 14–18 months | <12 months |
| LTV:CAC | <2x | 3–4x | >5x |
| Burn Multiple | >3x | 1.5–2.5x | <1.5x |
| Rule of 40 | <15 | 25–35 | >40 |
| Magic Number | <0.5 | 0.75–1.0 | >1.0 |
| SaaS Quick Ratio | <1.5 | 2–3 | >4 |

### How to Use Benchmarks in Conversations
- Present benchmarks in context: "Our NRR of 118% is above the Benchmarkit median of 108% and near top-quartile — this is a key differentiator"
- Don't cherry-pick: present the full picture; sophisticated investors will know the benchmarks
- Explain deviations: "Our CAC payback of 20 months is above median, but this reflects our enterprise motion where LTV is 7x CAC and churn is near zero"
- Show trends: A metric improving from 80th percentile to 90th percentile is more compelling than a single data point
