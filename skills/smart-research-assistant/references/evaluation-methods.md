# Source Evaluation Methods

## SIFT Method (Quick Verification)

### Stop
- Pause before using or sharing information
- Ask: Do I know this source? Do I have reason to trust it?

### Investigate
- Check source reputation and expertise
- Look for author credentials and organizational background
- Verify domain and website information

### Find
- Search for other coverage of the same claim
- Look for consensus among multiple sources
- Identify expert opinions on the topic

### Trace
- Go back to original source material
- Check for context and full quotes
- Verify that information hasn't been altered

## WWWDOT Framework (Detailed Analysis)

### Who wrote this?
- Author name and credentials
- Organizational affiliation
- Contact information
- Previous work and reputation

### Why was it written?
- Purpose and intent
- Target audience
- Potential biases
- Funding sources

### When was it written?
- Publication date
- Last updated
- Relevance to current context
- Timeliness of information

### Does this help meet my needs?
- Relevance to research question
- Depth of coverage
- Appropriateness for purpose
- Complementarity with other sources

### Organization of website
- Site structure and navigation
- Professional design and presentation
- Editorial standards
- Peer review processes

### To-do list for future
- Follow-up research needed
- Additional sources to consult
- Questions remaining unanswered
- Verification steps required

## CAPES Framework (Systematic Evaluation)

### Context
- Historical background
- Cultural and social context
- Political environment
- Industry landscape

### Actions
- Research methodology used
- Data collection processes
- Analysis techniques applied
- Verification steps taken

### Products
- Reports and documents generated
- Data sets compiled
- Visualizations created
- Conclusions drawn

### Evaluation
- Quality assessment criteria
- Reliability measures
- Validity checks
- Limitations identified

### Standards
- Academic standards applied
- Industry best practices
- Ethical guidelines followed
- Professional benchmarks met

## Lateral Reading Technique

### Process
1. Open multiple browser tabs
2. Research source while reading original content
3. Look for external validation
4. Check what other sources say about the original source

### Benefits
- Reduces confirmation bias
- Provides broader perspective
- Identifies potential conflicts of interest
- Establishes source reputation

## Algorithmic Awareness

### Understanding Algorithms
- How search engines rank results
- Social media content prioritization
- Personalization effects
- Filter bubble formation

### Mitigation Strategies
- Use multiple search engines
- Clear browsing history regularly
- Seek diverse perspectives
- Manually verify important claims
