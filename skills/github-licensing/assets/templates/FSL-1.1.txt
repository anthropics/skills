Functional Source License (FSL) 1.1 — fill-in guide

The FSL is a source-available license that automatically converts to a true
open-source license after TWO YEARS. It bars only "Competing Use"; everything
else (the "Permitted Purpose") is allowed.

Pick ONE future-conversion variant:
  - FSL-1.1-MIT     -> converts to the MIT License after 2 years
  - FSL-1.1-ALv2    -> converts to the Apache License 2.0 after 2 years

The ONLY fields you fill in are:
  ${year}            e.g. 2026
  ${licensor name}   e.g. Acme, Inc.

Everything else in the FSL text is FIXED — do not edit the Permitted Purpose,
Competing Use, or Grant of Future License sections.

Fetch the canonical template for your chosen variant from:
  https://fsl.software/        (official)
  https://spdx.org/licenses/FSL-1.1-MIT.txt
  https://spdx.org/licenses/FSL-1.1-ALv2.txt

Write the completed text to the repo as LICENSE.md (FSL convention), substituting
only ${year} and ${licensor name}.

Note: FSL is NOT OSI-approved while restricted; call it "source-available" or
"Fair Source" (fair.io), not "open source", until it converts.
