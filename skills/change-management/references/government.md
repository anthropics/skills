# Government / Education Agency Change Management Reference

## Governance & Authority Structures
- **Legislative authority**: Statutory mandates may initiate or constrain change; agency cannot exceed statutory authority
- **Executive authority**: Governor/Mayor/Secretary directives; executive orders may set implementation deadlines non-negotiably
- **Regulatory frameworks**: ESSA, IDEA, ADA, Title VI, Title IX — changes must be assessed for federal compliance implications
- **Rulemaking process**: Regulatory changes require notice-and-comment periods (typically 30–60 days); administrative changes may not
- **OMB / Budget offices**: Resource-dependent changes must clear budget authorization cycles
- **Civil service / HR rules**: Reclassifications, position changes, or role restructuring governed by civil service regulations and union contracts (AFGE, AFSCME, SEIU, state-specific)

## Political Environment Considerations
- **Administration transitions**: Change initiatives may be paused, reversed, or accelerated by election cycles — build sustainability into policy and regulation, not political relationships
- **Legislative oversight**: Committees may request hearings on major agency changes — prepare testimony-ready summaries
- **Interagency coordination**: Multi-agency changes require MOU/IAA frameworks and designated lead agency
- **Public comment & transparency**: FOIA exposure; assume all internal communications are discoverable — write accordingly

## Federal vs. State vs. Local Dynamics
| Level | Key Actors | Change Authority | Common Constraints |
|---|---|---|---|
| Federal (ED, DOL, HHS) | Secretary, Deputy, Office Directors | Regulatory / sub-regulatory guidance | APA rulemaking; Congressional notification |
| State Education Agency (SEA) | State Superintendent, Commissioner | State policy, ESSA State Plan amendments | Legislative session calendars; USED approval |
| Local Education Agency (LEA) | Superintendent, Board | Local policy, operational | Board approval cycles; CBA requirements |
| City/County Agency | Director, Deputy Director | Administrative | Procurement rules; civil service |

## Civil Service & Union Constraints
- Position reclassification: 60–180 days; may require union notification
- Telework/scheduling policy changes: typically subject to bargaining
- Technology system changes affecting job duties: may trigger impact bargaining obligations
- RIF (reduction in force) procedures: strict timelines and notice requirements

## Effective Engagement Channels
| Audience | High-Trust Channels | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Agency Staff | All-hands with Q&A, manager cascade, intranet | Town halls with anonymous Q&A reduce fear |
| Legislators / Committees | Briefings, one-pagers, testimony | Keep technical; anticipate adversarial framing |
| Regulated entities (LEAs, grantees) | Technical assistance webinars, FAQs, listservs | Provide compliance clarity before enforcement |
| Public / Advocacy groups | Public comment process, stakeholder convenings | Document engagement for rulemaking record |
| Federal partners | Interagency workgroups, ED program offices | Critical for ESSA/IDEA-linked changes |

## Common Resistance Patterns
- **Career staff skepticism of political appointees**: Especially for changes perceived as ideologically driven
  - *Mitigation*: Ground change in data, statutory authority, and career staff co-design
- **Compliance-over-improvement culture**: Staff default to "what's legally required" rather than "what works"
  - *Mitigation*: Build continuous improvement language into policy; distinguish compliance floor from excellence ceiling
- **Procurement & IT bottlenecks**: Technology-driven changes stall in contracting and security review
  - *Mitigation*: Engage procurement and CISO/IT security early — treat as change stakeholders, not just vendors

## Alignment Touchpoints
- ESSA State Plan (SEA-level)
- IDEA Part B/C State Performance Plan (SPP/APR)
- Agency strategic plan / APG (Agency Priority Goals)
- OMB Circular A-11 (budget planning)
- GAO / OIG audit recommendations
- Federal program monitoring findings
- Executive Order compliance requirements
