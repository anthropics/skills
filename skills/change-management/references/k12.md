# K-12 Change Management Reference

## Governance & Authority Structures
- **Decision authority**: Superintendent → Cabinet → Principals → Department Heads → Teachers
- **Board of Education**: Approves policy; major changes may require board resolution or public hearing
- **Union contracts (CBAs)**: Changes affecting working conditions, evaluation, schedules, or roles may require meet-and-confer or formal bargaining — flag early
- **Title I/ESSA compliance**: Ensure change initiatives align with school improvement plans (SIPs), LEA consolidated applications, and ESSA evidence tiers (Tiers 1–4)
- **IDEA / Section 504**: Changes affecting special education programs, caseloads, or service delivery require IEP team and LEA-level review

## Change Fatigue Factors (K-12 Specific)
- Teachers average 3–5 major initiatives simultaneously — always assess current initiative load
- August/September and January are highest-bandwidth windows for launch
- Avoid launching major changes in testing windows (March–May in most states)
- Principal turnover (avg. 3–5 years) disrupts continuity — build change into systems, not people

## Effective Engagement Channels
| Audience | High-Trust Channels | Low-Trust Channels |
|---|---|---|
| Teachers | Department/grade-level PLCs, union reps, peer coaches | Top-down email blasts, mandatory all-hands only |
| Principals | Principal network meetings, 1:1 with superintendent | Compliance memos without context |
| Parents/Families | School councils, multilingual town halls, text/app platforms | English-only formal letters |
| Students (secondary) | Student advisory boards, focus groups | Announcements only |

## Common K-12 Resistance Patterns
- **Veteran teacher resistance**: Autonomy threat; "initiative fatigue"; distrust of top-down mandates
  - *Mitigation*: Involve as co-designers; honor institutional knowledge; create opt-in pilot phases
- **Principal resistance**: Accountability fear; capacity concerns; unclear role in change
  - *Mitigation*: Role clarity; principal PLN peer support; celebrate early adopter principals publicly
- **Union/association resistance**: CBA concerns; workload; precedent-setting fears
  - *Mitigation*: Engage union leadership early and proactively; frame as support, not surveillance

## Alignment Touchpoints
- School Improvement Plans (SIPs)
- District Strategic Plan
- ESSA Consolidated State Plan
- Title I Schoolwide Plans
- MTSS / RTI frameworks
- Educator evaluation systems (DANIELSON, MARZANO, state-specific)

## Timeline Constraints
- Board approval cycles: typically 30–60 days for policy changes
- CBA negotiation: 60–180 days minimum if contract language is implicated
- PD calendar: limited to ~8–12 days/year in most districts — plan accordingly
- School year calendar: 180 instructional days; change implementation must fit around these constraints
