# Change Management Skill

## Overview

This skill generates modular, evidence-based change management deliverables for educational and mission-driven organizations. Outputs can be used independently or assembled into a full Change Management Plan.

All deliverables are grounded in established change models. Model selection is context-driven — see **Model Selection Guide** below.

---

## Step 1: Intake — Diagnose Before Designing

Before producing any output, gather the following. Ask the user directly if not provided:

| Parameter | Key Questions |
|---|---|
| **Change type** | What is changing? (policy, technology, structure, curriculum, staffing, culture) |
| **Org type** | K-12 district/school, nonprofit, higher ed, government/education agency |
| **Scope** | Single site vs. multi-site; local vs. systemic |
| **Timeline** | How many weeks/months until full implementation? |
| **Urgency** | Is this externally mandated (compliance, funding) or internally driven? |
| **Sponsor** | Who owns the change? (superintendent, ED, provost, director) |
| **Stakeholders** | Who is affected? Who has decision authority? Who has informal power? |
| **Known resistance** | Are there known objectors, skeptics, or impacted groups? |
| **Prior attempts** | Has this change been tried before? What happened? |
| **Data available** | Any survey data, staff sentiment, readiness indicators available? |

If the user says "just build it," make reasonable assumptions, state them explicitly, and proceed.

---

## Step 2: Model Selection

Select the best-fit model based on intake. Apply one primary model; reference secondary models for specific components.

| Model | Best For | Avoid When |
|---|---|---|
| **Kotter's 8-Step** | Large-scale, leadership-driven transformation; multi-phase rollouts | Short timelines; single-team changes |
| **ADKAR (Prosci)** | Individual behavior change; staff adoption of new tools/processes | Structural/system redesign without direct staff behavior change |
| **Lewin (Unfreeze–Change–Refreeze)** | Foundational culture shifts; policy overhauls; long-horizon work | Fast-cycle, iterative change |
| **McKinsey 7-S** | Restructuring; alignment audits; org-wide coherence analysis | Tactical implementations; short initiatives |
| **Bridges' Transition Model** | Change that involves significant loss, role shifts, or identity disruption | Purely technical changes with no emotional component |

**Hybrid approach:** For complex education/nonprofit contexts, combine:
- **Kotter** for the macro leadership roadmap
- **ADKAR** for the staff-level adoption plan
- **Bridges** when roles are being eliminated, merged, or significantly altered

State your model selection and rationale before producing any deliverable.

## Step 3: Modular Deliverables

Produce outputs based on user request. Each module is independent; they compose into a full plan.

### Module A — Change Readiness Assessment

Produce a structured readiness profile across five dimensions:

| Dimension | Indicators to Assess | Rating (1–5) |
|---|---|---|
| Leadership Commitment | Sponsor visibility, resource allocation, message consistency | |
| Organizational Capacity | Staff bandwidth, prior change fatigue, infrastructure | |
| Communication Infrastructure | Channels available, trust in leadership, information flow | |
| Cultural Alignment | Values fit, psychological safety, norms around innovation | |
| Data & Accountability Systems | Metrics in place, feedback loops, baseline data availability | |

Include: summary rating, top 3 risks, top 3 enablers, recommended pre-launch actions.

---

### Module B — Stakeholder Engagement Plan

Build a stakeholder map and engagement strategy matrix:

**Stakeholder Map (Power × Interest Grid)**
- Plot key individuals/groups across four quadrants: Manage Closely / Keep Satisfied / Keep Informed / Monitor

**Engagement Strategy Matrix**

| Stakeholder Group | Role in Change | Current Stance | Desired Stance | Engagement Strategy | Owner | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| (e.g., Teachers) | Implementers | Skeptical | Champions | PLC sessions, peer leads | Principal | Biweekly |

Include: coalition-building tactics, early adopter identification, resistor mitigation approach.

---

### Module C — Communication Strategy Matrix

| Message | Audience | Key Concern Addressed | Channel | Timing | Sender | Format |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Why this change, why now | All staff | Urgency + context | All-hands + email | Week 1 | Superintendent/ED | Town hall + memo |
| What changes for you | Affected roles | Role clarity | Department meeting | Week 2 | Direct supervisor | FAQ + 1:1 |
| How you'll be supported | Frontline staff | Capacity/fear | PD session | Week 3 | Coach/trainer | Workshop |
| Progress update | All stakeholders | Trust + transparency | Newsletter | Monthly | Change lead | Data dashboard |

**Communication Design Principles (for education/nonprofit contexts):**
- Lead with *why*, anchor in mission
- Differentiate messages by role and concern, not just title
- Build in two-way feedback mechanisms (surveys, office hours, anonymous channels)
- Acknowledge loss and difficulty explicitly (Bridges)
- Cascade: executive → manager → frontline, not all-broadcast

---

### Module D — Implementation Roadmap

Produce a phased roadmap. Adapt phase names to the selected change model.

**Standard Phase Structure (Kotter-aligned; adapt labels for other models):**

| Phase | Duration | Key Activities | Milestones | Owner | Dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Establish Urgency & Coalition | Weeks 1–3 | Data presentation, stakeholder analysis, sponsor alignment | Guiding coalition formed | Exec sponsor | Readiness assessment complete |
| 2. Vision & Strategy | Weeks 3–5 | Co-design sessions, logic model, theory of change draft | Vision statement approved | Change lead + team | Coalition in place |
| 3. Communicate & Enable | Weeks 5–10 | PD delivery, communication cascade, pilot launch | 80% staff trained | HR / PD lead | Comms plan finalized |
| 4. Remove Barriers & Quick Wins | Weeks 8–14 | Feedback loops, barrier removal, celebrate early wins | 2–3 documented wins shared | Middle managers | Pilot data available |
| 5. Sustain & Scale | Month 4+ | Policy embedding, accountability systems, culture reinforcement | Change in org procedures/job descriptions | Leadership | Full rollout complete |

**Formatting note:** If the user requests a visual Gantt-style timeline, produce it as an HTML artifact with interactive milestone markers. Offer this option proactively.

---

### Module E — Resistance & Risk Register

| Resistance Source | Type (Active/Passive/Structural) | Root Cause | Impact (H/M/L) | Probability (H/M/L) | Mitigation Strategy | Early Warning Signal | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| (e.g., Veteran teachers) | Passive | Change fatigue; loss of autonomy | H | H | Peer champion model; co-design input | Absenteeism from PD; informal veto language | Dept head |

**Resistance typology for education/nonprofit contexts:**
- **Structural resistance**: Misaligned incentives, unclear roles, resource gaps — address systemically
- **Cultural resistance**: "We tried this before" / identity threat — address through narrative, voice, and co-design
- **Capacity resistance**: Staff are willing but overwhelmed — address through workload relief, phasing, and support
- **Political resistance**: Competing agendas, union dynamics, board opposition — address through coalition and negotiation

## Step 4: Full Change Management Plan Assembly

If the user requests a complete plan, assemble all five modules in this order:

1. Executive Summary (1 page: change rationale, model selected, timeline, sponsor)
2. Change Readiness Assessment (Module A)
3. Stakeholder Engagement Plan (Module B)
4. Communication Strategy Matrix (Module C)
5. Implementation Roadmap (Module D)
6. Resistance & Risk Register (Module E)
7. Evaluation & Sustainability Plan (Kirkpatrick Levels 1–4 applied to change adoption)
8. Appendices (raw data, stakeholder lists, glossary)

Produce as a structured Markdown document or DOCX file (read SKILL at `/mnt/skills/public/docx/SKILL.md` if DOCX output is requested).

---

## Step 5: Evaluation & Sustainability

For every plan, include a brief sustainability framework:

| Kirkpatrick Level | Change Management Application | Measurement Approach |
|---|---|---|
| L1 — Reaction | Stakeholder sentiment during transition | Pulse surveys (monthly) |
| L2 — Learning | Staff understanding of new process/policy | Pre/post knowledge checks |
| L3 — Behavior | Adoption rate; fidelity to new practice | Observation, walkthroughs, usage data |
| L4 — Results | Organizational outcomes tied to change goal | KPIs, outcome data, funder metrics |

Add: institutionalization checklist (policy embedded? roles updated? onboarding revised? leadership succession plan for the change?)

## Quality Standards

- All frameworks must be **actionable** (named owners, timelines, concrete steps)
- All tables must be **editable** — avoid prose-heavy outputs for structured data
- Language must be **plain enough for frontline staff** yet rigorous enough for executives
- Always **name the change model used** and briefly justify the selection
- Always **acknowledge the human side of change** — loss, identity, pace — regardless of change type
- Flag if the timeline provided is **unrealistic** for the scope of change described
