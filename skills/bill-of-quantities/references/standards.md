# BoQ Standards Reference

## RICS Standard Method of Measurement (SMM)

The Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) provides standard classifications for construction items.

### Codes & Sections

- **A:** Preliminaries
- **B:** Groundwork
- **C:** Structures
- **D:** Finishes
- **E:** Services (MEP)
- **F:** Furniture & Equipment
- **G:** External Works
- **H:** Drainage
- **I:** Transportation

### Unit Conventions

| Item Type | Unit | Abbreviation | Notes |
|-----------|------|--------------|-------|
| Linear | Meter | m | Length, perimeter |
| Area | Square Meter | m² | Flooring, walls, surfaces |
| Volume | Cubic Meter | m³ | Concrete, excavation, fill |
| Weight | Tonne | t or T | Steel, materials |
| Count | Number | No. | Individual items |
| Length | Millimeter | mm | Detail specs |
| Area | Square Millimeter | mm² | Small items |

## Standard Conversion Factors

### Concrete

- 1 m³ of concrete ≈ 2.4 tonnes (nominal)
- 1 m³ of reinforcement steel ≈ 7.85 tonnes

### Excavation & Fill

- 1 m³ of loose excavation ≈ 1.3 m³ bulked
- 1 m³ of fill compacted ≈ 0.85 m³ loose material
- Bulk density varies: clay (1.6-1.8 t/m³), sand (1.5-1.7 t/m³), rock (2.2-2.5 t/m³)

### Brick & Blockwork

- 1 m² of 200mm concrete blocks ≈ 12.5 blocks
- 1 m² of brick (230×110mm) ≈ 50-60 bricks
- Mortar: approx 0.03 m³ per m² of wall

### Steel

- Reinforcement bar density: 0.616 kg/m for 8mm, 0.888 kg/m for 10mm, etc.
- Structural steel density: 7850 kg/m³

## Common Building Dimensions

### Standard Room Heights

- Ground floor: 4.0-4.5m (including slab depth)
- Upper floors: 3.0-3.5m
- Commercial: 3.6-4.0m
- Industrial: 5.0-7.0m

### Door/Window Sizes

- Internal door: 900mm wide
- External door: 1000mm wide
- Typical window: 1.2m × 1.5m
- Curtain wall typical module: 1.5m × 1.5m

## Quantity Calculation Examples

### Wall Area Calculation

For a perimeter wall:

- Perimeter = sum of all sides
- Wall area = Perimeter × Height
- Example: 100m perimeter, 3m height = 300 m²

### Concrete Slab

- Area = Length × Width
- Volume = Area × Thickness
- Example: 50m × 40m × 0.15m = 300 m³

### Excavation

- Volume = Area × Depth
- Add 10% for bulking if loose material
- Example: 2000 m² area × 0.5m depth = 1000 m³

### Brick/Block Wall

- Bricks per m² = 1 / (brick length × brick height)
- For 230×110mm brick ≈ 50 bricks/m²
- Mortar per m² ≈ 0.03 m³

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Double counting** - Item appears in multiple sections
2. **Unit confusion** - m vs m² vs m³
3. **Missing finishes** - Extracting structure but forgetting paint, flooring
4. **Incomplete specs** - Item description insufficient for pricing
5. **Rounding errors** - Accumulation of rounding in large quantities
6. **Not accounting for waste** - No contingency for waste/breakage
7. **Missing services** - Forgetting electrical, plumbing in spaces
8. **Access/logistics ignored** - No space allowed for equipment, access
